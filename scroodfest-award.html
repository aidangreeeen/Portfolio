// About Scroodfest

Scroodfest is a local snowboard contest created by former members and friends of the Northstar snowboard team. Founded to build community, get people into the mountains, and host a competition centered entirely around creativity and style — not scores or rankings in the traditional sense.

The contest embodies the spirit of snowboarding at its core: progression, expression, and community. Designing and fabricating the award for Scroodfest was an opportunity to bring that same spirit into the workshop.

// Overview

Designed and fabricated a custom award for Scroodfest using CNC milling techniques. Led the entire process from concept to production — from initial CAD design through CNC programming and final machining — ensuring precision and a high-quality finish throughout.

The award was modeled in SolidWorks and machined on a Tormach PCNC 1100 CNC Mill. CAD drawings were translated directly into CNC toolpaths for efficient and accurate manufacturing in machined aluminum.

// Build Log

Build Log

Phase 01

Concept & Design

Developed the award concept in SolidWorks from initial sketch through detailed CAD model. Design balanced aesthetic appeal with machinability constraints on the Tormach PCNC 1100.

→ Developed award concept and form
→ Modeled award geometry in SolidWorks
→ Validated design against CNC machining constraints
→ Created detailed engineering drawings
Phase 02

CNC Programming

Translated SolidWorks CAD drawings into CNC toolpaths for the Tormach PCNC 1100. Programmed tool selection, feed rates, and cutting sequences for efficient and accurate aluminum machining.

→ Generated CNC toolpaths from SolidWorks drawings
→ Selected tooling for aluminum stock
→ Programmed feed rates and cutting sequences
→ Verified program with dry run simulation
Phase 03

Machining

Set up and operated the Tormach PCNC 1100 CNC Mill to machine the award from aluminum stock. Monitored the machining process to ensure dimensional accuracy and surface finish quality throughout.

→ Set up aluminum stock on Tormach PCNC 1100
→ Executed CNC program — monitored throughout
→ Verified critical dimensions during machining
→ Inspected surface finish at each operation
Phase 04

Finishing

Post-machining finishing operations to achieve the final surface quality and appearance of the award.

→ Deburred all machined edges
→ Surface finishing to final specification
→ Final dimensional inspection
→ Award completed and delivered
